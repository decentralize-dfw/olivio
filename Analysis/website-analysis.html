1. Brand Overview & Positioning

OLIVIO & CO is a Mediterranean-inspired family eyewear brand that sells children's sunglasses, sport glasses, blue-light screen glasses, ski goggles, and accessories. The brand was founded under the design studio Yellowdot Design and has accumulated a notable portfolio of international design awards. Its dual-market structure operates two separate Shopify stores: olivioandco.com for the US market (pricing in USD, free shipping above $59) and olivioandco.eu for Europe (free shipping above €69). A dedicated B2B wholesale platform exists at b2b.olivioandco.com.

Award Credentials (verified)

  • 2020 Red Dot Award — Packaging Design (Eco-Packaging)
  • 2021 Junior Design Awards — Best Travel Products, Bronze
  • 2021 iF Design Award — Kids Sunglasses, Junior Sunglasses, Eco-Packaging
  • 2021 D&AD Awards — Shortlist, Packaging Design
  • 2022 ADC 101st Annual Awards — Bronze Cube + two Merit awards
  • 2022 iF Design Award — Screen Sunglasses
  • 2022 Green Product Award — Best of 2022, Kids category
The brand has genuine, verifiable design credentials. This is a strong differentiator that is currently underused on the website.

Key Brand Pillars (as stated on site)

  • Mediterranean lifestyle inspiration
  • Eco-conscious packaging (100% biodegradable sugarcane pulp, recycled PET microfiber cloth)
  • UV400 protection for children's sensitive eyes
  • Universal Fit nose bridge + flexible TR90/TPEE frames
  • Family-inclusive range (toddler 0–2y through teen & adult)

2.2 Issues Found

  • CRITICAL: On initial site load, a country selector modal (US / Europe) blocks the entire homepage. There is no automatic geolocation — every visitor is interrupted regardless of where they come from. This is a significant first-impression friction point.
  • BUG: The search functionality on the US store displays "Something went wrong. Please try again!" immediately upon loading — the search box is broken on page load.
  • TYPO: Collection URLs contain typos: /collections/sungalsses-oval and /collections/sungalsses-round (misspelled "sunglasses" in the URL slug — "sungalsses"). This affects SEO.
  • MISSING: No dedicated "New Arrivals" page in navigation — this is referenced in the mega-menu banner image but links to the generic "All Sunglasses" collection.
  • INCONSISTENCY: "CREATIVE DEDITION" is misspelled (should be "EDITION") on the homepage carousel banner text. This is visible to all users.

3. Homepage Analysis

3.1 What Exists

The homepage uses a full-width hero carousel with multiple collection banners. Confirmed sections include:

  • Hero slider: Art of Breakfast / Classic Collection / Creative Edition D
  • Promotional banner: Silicone Accessories Set — free with $89+ sunglasses orders
  • Brand inspiration section: "Inspired by the Mediterranean"
  • Collection grid with lifestyle imagery

3.2 Missing from Homepage

  • No above-the-fold value proposition. A new visitor has no immediate understanding of what makes OLIVIO & CO different. There is no tagline or USP statement in the hero area — only a collection name and "Shop Now" CTA.
  • No customer reviews or star ratings visible on the homepage. Social proof is absent from the most-trafficked page on the site.
  • Award badges are not displayed anywhere on the homepage. Red Dot, iF Design, and ADC awards — which are exceptionally credible — are buried on the About Us page only.
  • No trust badges (secure checkout, payment icons, UV certification marks, SSL notice) visible above the fold or in homepage sections.
  • No "As Seen In" press section. If the brand has had media coverage, it is not showcased.
  • No email capture / newsletter popup or inline sign-up with incentive. Email list building is not visible.
  • No bestseller product carousel with prices. The homepage shows collection banners, but no individual products with pricing or ratings — users must click into a collection before seeing any products.

4.3 Product Page Issues

  • No visible customer reviews or star ratings on product pages. The absence of a review system (e.g., Yotpo, Judge.me, Stamped.io) is one of the most significant conversion killers on the site. Parents purchasing eyewear for children heavily rely on peer validation.
  • No size guide link visible from product listings. (A size guide exists at the B2B site but its presence on the main US store product pages could not be confirmed as prominent.)
  • No "Recently Viewed" or "You May Also Like" product recommendation widget confirmed.
  • No wishlist functionality confirmed on the US store. For a gifting category (parents buying for kids, grandparents buying gifts), wishlists are a high-value feature.
  • No bundle builder or "Complete the Look" cross-sell. Accessories (O-ring chains, silicone sets) could be cross-sold directly from sunglasses product pages.
  • POSITIVE: The brand offers clear age-based navigation (Shop by Age), which is highly appropriate for the category and reduces purchase confusion for parents.
  • POSITIVE: Product naming is consistent and branded (e.g., "Classic Olivio Sand Beige", "Coral Reef Purple Coral").

5. Trust, Social Proof & Credibility

5.1 What Exists

  • Award credentials on About Us page (verified: Red Dot, iF Design, ADC, D&AD, Green Product Award)
  • Eco-sustainability claim: biodegradable sugarcane pulp packaging, recycled PET cloth
  • B2B wholesale platform (signals scale and trade credibility)
  • Customer service email: e-service-eu@olivioandco.com (EU) / US equivalent implied
  • FAQ page with order tracking, returns, and account support

5.2 What Is Missing

  • No customer reviews system on the website. No star ratings, no text reviews, no photo reviews. For a brand selling children's products at $20–$60+ per unit, the absence of reviews is a major trust deficit.
  • No Trustpilot profile confirmed for olivioandco.com.
  • Award logos are not displayed on product pages, homepage, or checkout. These credentials should be displayed prominently — they are the brand's strongest trust signal.
  • No UV certification or lens standard badges (CE, UV400, ANSI Z80.3) prominently displayed. Parents buying for children specifically look for these certifications.
  • No press/media mentions section.
  • No customer photo gallery (UGC feed) on the site.
  • No influencer or mommy-blogger partnership showcase.

8. E-Commerce & Conversion Optimization

8.1 Checkout & Cart

  • POSITIVE: Free shipping threshold clearly communicated in the header bar ($59 for US / €69 for EU).
  • POSITIVE: Guest checkout available — registration is not required.
  • POSITIVE: Discount code field present at checkout.
  • POSITIVE: Free silicone accessories set offered with $89+ sunglasses purchase — good bundle incentive.
  • No cart abandonment recovery mechanism visible (no popup exit-intent, no "save your cart" email trigger mentioned).
  • No upsell or cross-sell shown in the cart — accessories and screen glasses could be cross-sold here.
  • Order modification not available after submission — this is standard, but the FAQ communicates it bluntly, which could deter confidence before checkout.

8.2 Promotions & Pricing Strategy

  • Single-use promo codes supported.
  • Only one discount code per order — no stacking. (Communicated in FAQ.)
  • No loyalty rewards program. Repeat customers (parents buying for growing kids, new seasons) have no incentive structure beyond the product.
  • No subscription or replenishment model (relevant for accessories like replacement straps or cleaning cloths).
  • Seasonal promotions not visible on the US homepage at time of analysis.

8.3 Conversion Blockers

  1. Country-selector modal on every visit — forces a decision before any product is seen.
  2. Broken search on page load — "Something went wrong" error appears immediately.
  3. No customer reviews — arguably the single largest conversion blocker for first-time buyers.
  4. Award credentials hidden on About Us — trust signals not in the purchase flow.
  5. No recently viewed / comparison feature — makes it hard to evaluate options across collections.

  • The country-selector modal will appear on mobile as well, blocking the entire viewport before any content is visible.

15. Prioritized Recommendations

🔴 HIGH PRIORITY — Fix Immediately

1. Fix the broken search function. "Something went wrong" appears on page load and creates immediate negative first impressions. This must be debugged in the Shopify theme.
2. Implement a customer review system. Install Yotpo, Judge.me, or Stamped.io to collect and display star ratings and photo reviews. Activate post-purchase automated review request emails. This is the single highest-impact conversion improvement available.
3. Display award badges prominently. Red Dot, iF Design, ADC, and Green Product Award logos should appear on the homepage (below the hero), on product pages (near the Add to Cart button), and in the footer. These are world-class credentials and should not be hidden.
4. Replace or optimize the country-selector modal. Use IP-based geolocation (MaxMind or Shopify Markets) to auto-detect and route users. Present a dismissible banner rather than a full-screen modal blocker.
5. Fix URL typos. Redirect /sungalsses-oval → /sunglasses-oval and /sungalsses-round → /sunglasses-round. These hurt SEO and look unprofessional if shared.
6. Fix the homepage banner typo: "CREATIVE DEDITION" → "CREATIVE EDITION."

🟡 MEDIUM PRIORITY — Implement within 60 days

7. Add a homepage value proposition statement in the hero section: e.g., "Award-winning eyewear for kids who don't sit still — UV400 protection, flexible frames, eco-friendly." This should appear above the fold without requiring a click.
8. Create unique meta descriptions for each collection page. The current duplicate meta description ("Our trendy toddler sunglasses…") appears on dozens of pages. Write unique, keyword-targeted descriptions for each collection and age group.
9. Add an Instagram shoppable gallery to the homepage using a tool like LikeShop, Instafeed, or Foursixty. This creates social proof and UGC visibility without requiring a review system.
10. Implement a wishlist feature (Shopify apps: Wishlist Plus, Swym). Parents frequently research and save items before purchasing — particularly for gifting occasions.
11. Add UV certification and lens standard badges to product pages. Include CE mark, UV400 certification, and material safety information. Parents of young children make safety-first purchase decisions.
12. Launch a loyalty rewards program. Implement Smile.io or Yotpo Loyalty. Offer points for purchases, reviews, social shares, and referrals. Given the product ladder (toddler → kids → junior → teen), parents are a natural multi-year repeat customer base.
13. Publish new blog content monthly — particularly buying guides and seasonal content (summer sun protection, back to school screen glasses, ski goggle guide). Target high-intent commercial keywords in each article.

🟢 LOW PRIORITY / GROWTH — Implement within 90–180 days

14. Launch a TikTok presence targeting parents (aged 28–42) via authentic family lifestyle content. Unboxing, "does my toddler keep them on?" content, and outdoor adventure videos are highly shareable in this category.
15. Create a dedicated Sustainability page (/pages/sustainability) showcasing the sugarcane packaging, recycled PET cloth, material choices, and future commitments. This supports both brand trust and SEO.
16. Add a "Find a Retailer" locator page linking to physical retail partners who carry OLIVIO & CO products. This serves customers who prefer in-store purchase and validates the brand's distribution reach.
17. Add Product Schema markup to all product pages for rich snippet display in Google search results (star ratings, price, availability). This requires either a Shopify app or theme-level JSON-LD implementation.
18. Create a branded hashtag campaign (e.g., #OlivioMoments or #MediterraneanKids) and feature UGC submissions on the website and social media. Incentivize with a monthly feature or small reward.
19. Introduce cross-sell on product pages. Add "Complete the Look" or "You Might Also Like" sections that suggest accessories (O-ring chains, silicone sets) when viewing sunglasses, and vice versa.
20. Consider merging the US and EU stores into a single international Shopify Markets store with currency localization, to eliminate the forced-choice modal and streamline SEO authority into one domain.
